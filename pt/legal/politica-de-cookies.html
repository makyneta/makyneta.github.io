01 — Definition

What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device — computer, tablet, or smartphone — when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, to remember your preferences, and to provide information to the owners of the site.

Similar technologies, such as web beacons, pixel tags, and local storage, operate in comparable ways and are also covered by this policy. Throughout this document, the term "cookies" refers collectively to all such technologies.

Session cookies Temporary cookies that are deleted when you close your browser. They are used to maintain state during a single visit.
Persistent cookies Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted. They are used to remember preferences across visits.
First-party cookies Cookies set directly by Makyneta on this website.
Third-party cookies Cookies set by external services integrated into this website, such as analytics or embedded content providers.

04 — Consent & Control

Your Choices

When you first visit this website, you will be presented with a cookie notice that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. Strictly necessary cookies do not require consent as they are essential for the website to function.

You may withdraw or change your consent at any time by clearing your browser cookies and revisiting the site, or by using the browser controls described in the following section.

Refusing non-essential cookies will not prevent you from using any core feature of this website. It may affect certain preferences that are stored locally between visits.

06 — Retention

Data Retention

Cookies and the data they carry are retained only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purpose for which they were set. Session cookies are deleted automatically when you close your browser. Persistent cookies expire after the period stated in our cookie table, unless you delete them earlier through your browser settings.

Data collected through analytics cookies is stored in aggregated, anonymised form and is not linked to any individual identity. It is retained for no longer than 26 months, in line with guidance from supervisory authorities.
